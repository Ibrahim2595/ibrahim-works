I’m Ibrahim – a computational designer, architect and engineer. I study Design Technology Mediums: On Making Culture, Technology and Art, at the Harvard Graduate School of Design in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

My work focuses on creative machines, computational geometry and creative applications of machine learning and artificial intelligence in design tools and processes.

I concept, design, program and make real the things I want to see in the world. These objects and bits of code are spurred to adapt, augment, and transform the surroundings into something other than what was before. I am not devoted to one particular medium or way of making, but explore creative opportunities at the intersection of design, technology, machines, robots, fabrication and data in both the virtual and real environments.

I have studied architecture and worked as a project designer and planner at m0rphosis architects in Los Angeles, designing and delivering the 170km LINE project in NEOM, KSA.
